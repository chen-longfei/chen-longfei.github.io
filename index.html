About Me

I am a passionate researcher specializing in Computer Vision, Statistical Learning (Temporal Modeling), and Human Behavior Analysis. Currently, I am a visitor at the School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh, having previously served as a Postdoctoral Research Associate (2021–2025). I earned my PhD in Electrical Engineering from Kyoto University in 2020.

My work centers on vision-based physical behavior analysis, anomaly detection, temporal modelling, and activity recognition. I am driven by the challenge of developing privacy-preserving methodologies for continuous human monitoring. I am multilingual (fluent in English and Chinese, with intermediate Japanese) and actively seeking opportunities to bring my expertise in deep learning and statistical modeling to innovative teams.

Featured Research Projects

Vision-Based Human Physical Behavior Analysis (2021–2025)

Developed privacy-preserving methods for sensing physical behavior in domestic environments. My work utilized RGB-D imaging, activity recognition, behavior analysis, and temporal modeling (Bayesian Networks, Gaussian Processes) to infer changes in behavioral states. Key contributions include handling real-world challenges like low lighting and moving pets to reliably detect anomalies, inactivity, and subtle functional decline.

Human Behavior Analysis Temporal Modeling Gaussian Processes Anomaly Detection Change Point Detection

Analysis and Modeling of Machine Operation Tasks using Egocentric Vision (2015–2020)

Investigated operational behaviors using head-mounted cameras, IMUs, and gaze trackers. I developed methods for touch sensing and hotspot detection, and applied Modified Hidden Markov Models to model complex task sequences (repetitive, mistakes, new ways) for automatic guidance systems and user skill assessment.

Egocentric Vision Activity Recognition HMMs
