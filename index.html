Research Projects

Vision-Based Human Physical Behavior Analysis for Potential Health Conditions (2021‚Äì2025)

University of Edinburgh

Developed privacy-preserving methods to support independent living for older adults in homes. My work utilized RGB-D imaging, activity recognition (CNN, VAE, RNN/LSTM), behavior analysis (fine-grained body movement features), and temporal modeling (Bayesian Networks, Gaussian Processes) to infer changes in health state in domestic environments. Key contributions include handling real-world challenges like low lighting and moving pets to reliably detect inactivity, and modeling temporal variances for detecting subtle functional decline.

Analysis and Modeling of Machine Operation Tasks using Egocentric Vision (2015‚Äì2020)

Kyoto University (PhD Thesis)

Investigated operational behaviors using head-mounted cameras, IMUs, and gaze trackers. I developed methods for touch sensing and hotspot detection, and applied Modified Hidden Markov Models to model complex task sequences (repetitive, mistakes, new ways, degree of freedom) for automatic guidance systems and user skill assessment.
